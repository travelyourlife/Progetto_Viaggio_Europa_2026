# Norway Nature Research Notes

## GEOLOGY
- Norway sits on the Baltic Craton (Baltic Shield) - one of Earth's most ancient cratons
- Lofoten bedrock: ~3 billion years old (among oldest in Europe) - gneiss and granite
- Western Gneiss Region - Lofoten is a horst ridge of bedrock
- Caledonian mountain range ~425 million years ago - peaks up to 10,000m (now eroded)
- Fjords: U-shaped valleys carved by glaciers during ice ages, filled with seawater when glaciers retreated
- Glaciers picked up debris, carved valleys below sea level
- Fjords deepest further inland (debris settles at glacier terminus)
- Cold-water coral reefs in fjord depths (Røst Reef off Lofoten: 43 km long, world's largest deep-sea coral reef)
- Tautra sill (Trondheim Fjord): shallowest known cold-water coral reefs at 39m depth (Lophelia pertusa)
- Moskstraumen (Maelstrom): one of world's strongest tidal currents between Lofoten islands
  - Generated by large tidal amplitude differences between Vestfjord and Norwegian Sea
  - Creates large whirlpools/eddies midway between high and low tide

## FLORA
- Boreal forest: Scots pine (furu), Norway spruce (gran), birch (bjørk)
- Mountain birch (Betula pubescens subsp. czerepanovii) - dominates Northern Boreal (100-400m in Troms)
- Downy birch (Betula pubescens) - main deciduous tree
- Alpine/tundra vegetation above treeline: dwarf shrubs, lichens, mosses, grasses
- Lofoten flora: surprisingly diverse due to Gulf Stream warming
  - Wild berries: blueberries, cranberries, crowberries, cloudberries, lingonberries
  - Coastal meadows with wildflowers
- Endemic mosses: Sphagnum troendelagicum, Schistidium bryhnii
- Mushrooms: chanterelles (kantarell), birch bolete (brunskrubb), orange birch bolete (rødskrubb), porcini
- Arctic-alpine plants: mountain avens, purple saxifrage, moss campion

## FAUNA

### Marine mammals
- Orcas (killer whales): 300+ gather near Lofoten/Vesterålen (following herring)
- Humpback whales: up to 600 in winter
- Sperm whales: year-round off Vesterålen (Andenes), visible under midnight sun
- Minke whales, pilot whales, porpoises
- Harbour seals and grey seals along coast
- Otters along coastal zone

### Land mammals
- Moose/elk: present in Lofoten forests
- Red fox, Arctic fox (endangered on mainland, thriving in Svalbard)
- Musk ox: Dovrefjell (only wild population in Scandinavia)
- Reindeer: semi-wild herds in Lapland/Finnmark, herded by Sámi people
- Stoat/ermine, hare
- Brown bear, wolverine, lynx, wolf (rare, mainly eastern Norway)

### Birds
- White-tailed sea eagle (havørn): 2m wingspan, large population in Lofoten
- Atlantic puffin: colonies on Røst (largest in Norway - 25% of country's seabirds)
- Golden eagle, peregrine falcon
- Ptarmigan (rype) - national game bird
- Whooper swan, Arctic tern, black guillemot, kittiwake
- Eurasian oystercatcher, curlew, redshank
- Cormorants, gannets, razorbills
- Great tit, chaffinch, bullfinch, woodpeckers in birch forests

### Fish
- Atlantic cod (skrei): migrates from Barents Sea to Lofoten to spawn (Jan-Mar)
- Stockfish (tørrfisk): air-dried cod, Viking tradition, built Bergen's economy
- Halibut, haddock, wolffish, pollock
- Coastal cod depend on kelp forests

## ECOSYSTEMS
- Kelp forests (Laminaria hyperborea): dominant coastal ecosystem
  - "Rainforests of the sea" - spawning/nursery for fish
  - 2,000+ km² destroyed by sea urchin bloom in 1970s
  - Restoration: 21 million urchins removed in Porsangerfjord
  - Red king crab (invasive) helps control urchins
- Boreal forest (inland): pine, spruce, birch
- Alpine tundra (above treeline): lichens, dwarf shrubs
- Coastal meadows and wetlands
- Deep fjord ecosystems: cold-water corals, unique deep-sea life

## NATURAL PHENOMENA
- Midnight sun in Lofoten: May 26 - July 17 (68°N)
  - Sun doesn't set below horizon for ~52 days
- Northern lights (aurora borealis): NOT visible in summer (too bright)
  - Best: September-March in Northern Norway
- Moskstraumen (Maelstrom): tidal whirlpools
- Polar night (mørketid): Nov-Jan in Northern Norway (sun doesn't rise)
- Noctilucent clouds visible in summer

## CULTURAL CONNECTIONS
- Stockfish → lutefisk (lye-soaked, Christmas tradition)
- Sámi reindeer herding
- Jokamiehenoikeus equivalent: Allemannsretten (right to roam)
- Cod fishing shaped entire coastal culture for 1000+ years
