# Mushroom Identification Research Notes

## Chanterelle (Cantharellus cibarius / kantarelli)
- **Appearance**: Egg-yolk yellow to pale orange/peach, funnel/vase shape, 2-10 cm cap
- **Underside**: Fork-like RIDGES (not true gills), thick, widely spaced, blunt edges, extend down stem, same color as cap or slightly paler
- **Stem**: Dense, thick, same color as cap, WHITE flesh inside. Breaks like string cheese (peelable layers)
- **Smell**: Pleasant fruity/APRICOT aroma (key identifier!)
- **Habitat**: Grows FROM THE GROUND (never from wood), coniferous/mixed/birch forests, prefers bright areas, well-drained soils
- **Season in Finland**: End of August to September
- **Cooking**: Especially delicious fried in butter, good in pizzas/omelettes. Can be frozen but gets leathery when dried.

### vs FALSE CHANTERELLE (Hygrophoropsis aurantiaca)
- **Cap**: Brighter orange (especially when young), darker in center/lighter edges, VELVETY/fuzzy surface (key!)
- **Gills**: TRUE thin gills (not ridges), more crowded, easily separated/scraped off, sharp edges, brighter than cap
- **Stem**: Same color as cap but snaps cleanly (doesn't peel like string cheese), finely fuzzy
- **Smell**: Generic earthy mushroom smell (NO apricot)
- **Habitat**: Grows on/from decomposing wood (stumps, wood chips, buried wood)
- **Toxicity**: Not deadly but causes gastrointestinal distress

### Quick ID checklist:
1. Cap smooth (not fuzzy)? → True chanterelle
2. Ridges (not thin gills) underneath? → True chanterelle
3. Stem breaks in peelable layers? → True chanterelle
4. Apricot smell? → True chanterelle
5. Growing from ground (not wood)? → True chanterelle

## Porcini / Penny Bun (Boletus edulis / herkkutatti)
- **Cap**: Convex to slightly flattened, brown/dark brown, 8-25 cm, smooth, slightly sticky when wet
- **Pores**: Small, densely packed, white → yellowish → greenish as mature
- **Stem**: Thick, bulbous, white to light brown, WHITE NET PATTERN (reticulation) visible under cap
- **Flesh**: White, doesn't change color when cut, firm, nutty flavor
- **Habitat**: Deciduous and coniferous forests, July to October
- **Finnish saying**: Check for white net on stem under cap = penny bun confirmed

### vs BITTER BOLETE (Tylopilus felleus / sappitatti)
- **Cap**: Uniformly light brown to brown (not dark brown like cep)
- **Pores**: Pale cream → PINKISH (key difference!)
- **Stem**: DARK net pattern (vs white in cep) - "dark fishnet stockings"
- **Flesh**: May turn slightly pink when cut
- **Taste**: Extremely bitter (even raw) - ruins entire dish if one gets in
- **Finnish saying**: "Bitter bolete is a naughty girl with dark fishnet stockings and light pink miniskirt"
- **Not poisonous** but inedible due to bitterness

### Quick ID checklist:
1. White net on stem? → Cep (dark net = bitter bolete)
2. White/yellowish/greenish pores? → Cep (pink pores = bitter bolete)
3. Taste mild/nutty? → Cep (bitter = bitter bolete)

## Funnel/Trumpet Chanterelle (Craterellus tubaeformis / suppilovahvero)
- **Cap**: Brown with yellowish or grey hue, funnel/horn shaped, small
- **Stem**: Hollow, slim, YELLOW color (key identifier)
- **Underside**: Long gill-like yellowish-grey ridges (decurrent)
- **Habitat**: Mossy woods, spruce stands, mixed forests. Grows in LARGE GROUPS same places yearly. Covered in deep moss.
- **Season**: September to November (later than chanterelle)
- **Cooking**: Thin flesh → excellent dried (easy at home). Rich in vitamin D. Good in stews/sauces.
- **Lookalike**: Wetland Chanterelle (C. aureum) - equally edible, less pronounced ridges, moister habitat

## Hedgehog Mushroom (Hydnum repandum)
- **Most foolproof** mushroom to identify - NO poisonous lookalikes in the Hydnaceae family
- **Cap**: Light yellow, often weirdly/irregularly shaped
- **Underside**: TEETH (like toothbrush bristles) instead of gills - unique identifier
- **Stem**: All white
- **Season**: End of August to September, likes moist soil
- **Note**: Higher Cesium-137 from Chernobyl - can reduce by soaking/boiling

## Black Trumpet (Craterellus cornucopioides)
- **Appearance**: Black/grey, soft funnel shape, looks almost inedible
- **Habitat**: Groves or bright spruce forests, grows in armies
- **Season**: Early autumn (same as chanterelle)
- **Cooking**: Drying makes taste better (subtle fresh). Cut dirty stems.

## Orange Birch Bolete (Leccinum versipelle)
- **Cap**: Bright orange, tall mushroom
- **Flesh**: Turns purple/blue when cut
- **Must be cooked** 15-20 min (mildly toxic raw)
- **Turns dark** when cooked

## DEADLY: Destroying Angel (Amanita virosa)
- ALL WHITE mushroom
- Can kill an adult human
- Finnish rule: "Don't pick white mushrooms" (general safety rule)
