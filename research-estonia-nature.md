# Estonia Nature Research

## Flora
- ~50% territory covered in forests (pine, spruce, birch dominant)
- Bogs/wetlands: 6-7% actual coverage (22% if including all peat areas)
- Mushrooms: cultural cornerstone, "seenelkäik" (mushroom walk)
  - Top edible: Chanterelle (kukeseen), Porcini (kivipuravik), Bay Bolete, Birch Bolete, Saffron Milkcap, Slippery Jack, Funnel Chanterelle, Hedgehog Mushroom, Parasol, Oyster
  - Dangerous: Death Cap (Amanita phalloides), Destroying Angel (A. virosa), Fly Agaric
  - Season: Aug-Oct peak, Jun-Aug summer start
  - Everyman's Right (Igaüheõigus) allows foraging
- Berries: cloudberry (in bogs), bilberry, lingonberry, cranberry
- Bog plants: Sphagnum moss (absorbs 20x body mass), sundew (carnivorous)
- Trees: Scots pine, Norway spruce, silver/downy birch, oak (on islands), ash

## Fauna
- Large carnivores: Brown bear, Wolf, Eurasian Lynx (800! - very high density), Wolverine (rare)
- Flying squirrel (Pteromys volans) - only in Alutaguse, NE Estonia, very rare in EU
- Moose - prefers bogs and wetlands as habitat
- Wild boar, beaver, roe deer, red deer
- Grey seal (Halichoerus grypus) - 10,000+ in Estonian waters, breeds on ice/islands
- Ringed seal (in eastern Baltic)
- Birds: White stork (nests on electric poles), Black stork, Common crane, Eurasian eagle-owl
  - Matsalu National Park: one of most important wetland bird areas in Europe (East Atlantic Flyway)
  - 250+ plant species, 64 species of fish, millions of migratory birds
  - Barnacle geese, greylag geese, whooper swans pass through
- Fish: pike, perch, pikeperch, eel, salmon, trout, Baltic herring (kiluvõileib!)

## Geology
- Baltic Klint: 1100-1200 km long limestone escarpment, best developed in N. Estonia
  - Ordovician limestone (470-460 million years old)
  - UNESCO tentative list
  - Visible as dramatic coastal cliffs (up to 56m at Ontika)
- Glacial erratic boulders: Estonia famous for high concentration
  - Ehalkivi ("Sunset Glow Rock"): biggest in N. Europe, 7m high, 48.2m circumference, near Kunda
  - Majakivi: 7m high, 32m perimeter
  - Brought by glaciers from Scandinavia (Finnish/Swedish granite)
- Islands: 2,222 islands (Saaremaa, Hiiumaa largest)
  - Kaali meteorite crater on Saaremaa (3,500 years old, 110m diameter)
- Flat terrain: highest point Suur Munamägi only 318m

## Natural Phenomena
- White nights: sun sets ~11pm, rises ~4am around solstice at 59°N (Tallinn)
  - Never truly dark, sky glows, can read outside at midnight
  - ~19 hours of daylight at summer solstice
- No midnight sun (too far south, 59.4°N)
- Short winter days: ~6 hours daylight in December
- Northern lights occasionally visible in autumn/winter

## Ecosystems
- Boreal/hemiboreal forest (transition zone between temperate and boreal)
- Raised bogs: oldest 9,000-10,000 years, peat 5-7m thick, 1mm/year accumulation
  - Stages: lake → fen → transitional mire → raised bog
  - Sphagnum moss = "bog builder plant"
  - Bog pools, bog islands (oases with tall forest)
  - Soomaa National Park: "fifth season" (spring floods)
- Coastal meadows and alvars (thin soil on limestone, unique grassland)
- West Estonian archipelago: unique maritime ecosystem
