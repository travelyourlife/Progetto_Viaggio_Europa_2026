# Finland Nature Research Notes

## FLORA
- **Conifers dominate**: Scots pine (Pinus sylvestris) and Norway spruce (Picea abies) — 73% of land is forest
- **Deciduous zone** (extreme south): birch (silver & downy), hazel, aspen, maple, elm, linden, alder
- **Arctic/north**: dwarf arctic birch, pygmy willow, lichens increasingly common northward
- **Over 1,000 species of flowering plants** recorded
- **Berries** (Everyman's Rights — free foraging):
  - Bilberry/mirtillo selvatico (Vaccinium myrtillus) — ripens mid-July south, late July north
  - Lingonberry/mirtillo rosso (Vaccinium vitis-idaea) — ripens Aug-Sep
  - Cloudberry/camemoro (Rubus chamaemorus) — "gold of the bogs", ripens July in peatlands
  - Raspberry (Rubus idaeus)
  - Cranberry (Vaccinium oxycoccos) — bogs
  - Crowberry (Empetrum nigrum) — Lapland
  - Sea buckthorn (Hippophae rhamnoides) — coastal
- **Mushrooms** (200+ edible species):
  - Chanterelle/gallinaccio (Cantharellus cibarius) — most popular
  - Porcini/porcino (Boletus edulis)
  - Milk caps (Lactarius spp.) — eastern Finnish tradition
  - Funnel chanterelle/finferla (Craterellus tubaeformis) — autumn
- **Aquatic plants**: White water lily (Nymphaea alba), Yellow water lily (Nuphar lutea) — common on Finnish lakes
- **Mosses & lichens**: Sphagnum mosses in bogs, reindeer lichen (Cladonia rangiferina) in north
- **Wildflowers**: Lily of the valley, bloody crane's-bill, blue bugle, marsh Labrador tea (Rhododendron tomentosum — fragrant white blooms in wetlands), Lapland rosebay (rare, purple-pink, Arctic)
- **National flower**: Lily of the valley (Convallaria majalis)

## FAUNA
### Mammals (Big 4 carnivores)
- **Brown bear** (Ursus arctos) — national animal, ~2,000 individuals, Eastern Finland
- **Wolf** (Canis lupus) — Eastern Finland, near Russian border, elusive
- **Wolverine** (Gulo gulo) — North Karelia (Lieksa), one of best places in world to see them
- **Lynx** (Lynx lynx) — throughout Finnish forests, very secretive

### Other mammals
- **Elk/moose** (Alces alces) — up to 700 kg, found everywhere, active dawn/dusk
- **Reindeer** — ~200,000 semi-domesticated in Lapland, adapted to -50°C, see UV light
- **Saimaa ringed seal** — ONLY in Lake Saimaa, ~530 individuals (up from <200 in 1980s), recognized as own species 2025, endangered, isolated from Baltic 8,000 years ago after Ice Age
- **Flying squirrel** (Pteromys volans) — nocturnal, old-growth forests
- **Arctic fox** — Lapland
- **Beaver** — lakes and rivers

### Birds
- **Whooper swan** (national bird) — returns spring, nests on lakeshores
- **White-tailed eagle** — wingspan 2.5m, Europe's largest eagle, Baltic coast
- **Great grey owl** — world's longest owl, northern/central forests
- **Siberian jay** — bold, sociable, Lapland forests
- **Black grouse, Capercaillie, Hazel grouse** — game birds
- **Osprey** — fish-hunting raptor over lakes
- **Black-throated loon** — iconic lake bird
- **Goldeneye** — Finland is breeding site for majority of world's goldeneyes

### Fish
- Salmon, trout, whitefish (siika) — northern rivers
- Baltic herring — most common sea fish
- Pike, perch, char — lakes
- Crayfish — brief summer season
- Zander (kuha) — prized lake fish

## GEOLOGY
- **Bedrock**: Fennoscandian Shield, 1.9–1.6 billion years old (Paleoproterozoic)
  - Orthogneiss, granite, metavolcanics, metasedimentary rocks
  - Rapakivi granite — distinctive "crumbly" granite, SE Finland & Åland
  - National stone: granite
- **Quaternary glaciation**:
  - Ice sheet 2 km thick covered all Finland ~25,000 years ago
  - Eroded ~25m of rock on average
  - Left: eskers, drumlins, moraines, till, varved clay
- **Eskers**: Ridges of sand/gravel deposited in meltwater tunnels inside glacier
  - Punkaharju Ridge — world-famous 7 km esker between two lakes
  - Longest esker chains in the world
- **Salpausselkä**: Two massive ice-marginal moraines (12,300–11,600 years ago)
- **Drumlins**: Droplet-shaped moraine ridges, formed by flowing glacier
- **Lake Saimaa**: 4th largest lake in Europe, formed by glacial meltwater filling depressions
  - 14,000+ islands, labyrinthine shape
  - Part of Saimaa UNESCO Global Geopark
- **Post-glacial rebound**: Land still rising ~5-9 mm/year (Ostrobothnia coast)
- **188,000 lakes** — formed in glacial depressions and between moraines/eskers

## NATURAL PHENOMENA
- **Midnight sun / Nightless night (yötön yö)**:
  - Above Arctic Circle: sun doesn't set May–August (73 days in far north)
  - Southern Finland (Helsinki): virtual daylight 24h, sun briefly dips below horizon
  - Lakeland (61°N): ~20h of daylight in summer, no true darkness June-July
  - Light takes reddish-yellow colour, like perpetual golden hour
- **White nights (valoisat yöt)**: Throughout Finland in summer
- **Noctilucent clouds**: Visible in Finnish summer skies (highest clouds, 80 km altitude, glow after sunset)
- **Aurora borealis**: Mainly autumn/winter/spring, visible ~200 nights/year in Lapland
  - In summer: too light to see, but active
- **Midsummer (Juhannus)**: Summer solstice celebration, bonfires
- **Ice phenomena** (winter): Not relevant for summer trip but worth noting

## ECOSYSTEMS / HABITATS
- **Boreal forest (taiga)**: Dominates 73% of Finland
  - Coniferous: pine + spruce, understory of bilberry, lingonberry, mosses
  - Mixed: birch + conifers in south
  - Old-growth forests: rare, protected in national parks
- **Peatlands/bogs**: ~30% of land (10 million hectares!)
  - Sphagnum bogs, fens, mires
  - Cloudberry habitat, cranberry
  - Important carbon stores
- **Lakeland ecosystem**: 188,000 lakes
  - Oligotrophic (nutrient-poor, clear water)
  - Fish: pike, perch, zander, whitefish
  - Birds: loons, grebes, goldeneye, osprey
  - Saimaa ringed seal habitat
- **Archipelago/coastal**: Baltic Sea coast
  - Brackish water, unique mix of freshwater/marine species
  - White-tailed eagle territory
- **Fell/tundra** (Lapland): Above treeline
  - Dwarf birch, lichens, mosses
  - Ptarmigan, Arctic fox, reindeer
- **Rivers**: Salmon rivers in north (Teno/Tana, Tornio)
