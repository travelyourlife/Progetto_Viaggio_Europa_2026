# Research Notes - All Remaining Countries

## LATVIA
- Gauja National Park: 350 million-year-old Devonian red sandstone cliffs, caves
  - 900+ plant species, 149 bird species, 48 mammal species
  - Eurasian otters, red deer, wild boar
- Large carnivores: lynx, wolves, moose, wild boar
- European bison reintroduced
- Black storks, corncrakes, lesser-spotted eagles
- Baltic coast: 496 km coastline, Jurmala dunes (15m high White Dunes)
- Baltic amber (fossilized resin, 40-50 million years old)
- Forests: ~52% of territory, pine/spruce/birch
- Kemeri National Park: sulfur springs, raised bogs

## LITHUANIA
- Curonian Spit: UNESCO World Heritage, 98 km sand dune peninsula
  - Separates Baltic Sea from Curonian Lagoon
  - Grand Dune Ridge, shifting white/grey dunes (up to 60m high)
  - Unique flora: Curonian rose, dune grasses, pine forests
- White Stork: national bird, ~13,000 breeding pairs (3rd largest in EU after Poland/Spain)
  - Densities up to 160 nests/100 km² in western Lithuania
- Nemunas Delta: maze of river branches, polders, wetlands
  - Important bird area, migratory route
- Forests: ~33% of territory
- Amber: "Lithuanian gold", Baltic amber deposits
- Dzūkija forests: mushroom picking tradition
- Wildlife: moose, deer, wild boar, beaver, wolf (small population)

## POLAND
- Białowieża Forest: UNESCO, last primeval forest in Europe
  - 800+ European bison (25% of world population)
  - 12,000+ animal species, ancient trees (oaks 500+ years)
  - Wolves, lynx, wild boar, deer, beaver
- Tatra Mountains: highest in Carpathians
  - Tatra chamois, Alpine marmot, wolf, brown bear, lynx, golden eagle
  - Edelweiss, gentian, alpine flora
- Słowiński National Park: moving sand dunes on Baltic coast (40m+ high)
  - UNESCO Biosphere Reserve
  - "Polish Sahara" - dunes move 3-10m per year
- Biebrza Marshes: largest wetland in Central Europe
- Storks: Poland has largest white stork population in EU

## AUSTRIA
- Alpine flora/fauna: iconic species
  - Edelweiss (Leontopodium nivale) - protected, symbol of Alps
  - Gentian (Gentiana) - blue flowers, used for schnapps
  - Alpenrose (Rhododendron ferrugineum) - pink alpine shrub
  - Alpine ibex (Capra ibex) - steep rocky terrain, 100kg
  - Chamois (Rupicapra rupicapra) - agile mountain goat-antelope
  - Alpine marmot (Marmota marmota) - whistling, hibernates 6 months
  - Golden eagle (Aquila chrysaetos) - 2m wingspan
  - Alpine salamander - entirely black, lives at altitude
- Grossglockner: highest peak (3,798m), Pasterze glacier
- Geology: Alps formed by collision of African/European plates
  - Limestone Alps, crystalline Central Alps
  - Karst formations, caves (Eisriesenwelt - largest ice cave)
- Danube wetlands: Donau-Auen National Park
- Forests: ~47% of territory, spruce dominant

## DENMARK
- Wadden Sea: UNESCO World Heritage
  - 12-15 million migratory birds on East Atlantic flyway
  - 400+ bird species, harbour seals, grey seals
  - Tidal flats, mudflats, salt marshes
  - "Sort Sol" (Black Sun): starling murmurations
- Geology: entirely shaped by Ice Age glaciation
  - Moraine landscape, no mountains (highest point 171m)
  - Chalk cliffs of Møns Klint (70m, 70 million year old chalk)
  - Erratic boulders from Scandinavia
- Beech forests: UNESCO World Heritage (ancient beech forests)
  - European beech dominant, arrived 3,500 years ago
- Red deer: largest wild land animal in Denmark
- Marine: harbour porpoise, seals, cod, herring
- Rold Skov: largest forest, springs
- Skagen: where two seas meet (Kattegat/Skagerrak)

## FRANCE (Atlantic coast, Brittany, Normandy)
- Brittany coast: 2,800 km (longest in France)
  - Common bottlenose dolphin, basking shark, grey seal
  - Atlantic puffin colonies (Sept-Îles)
  - Seabird colonies: gannets, cormorants, guillemots
- Normandy: chalk cliffs of Étretat
  - Tidal ecosystems, oyster beds
- Atlantic coast: Bay of Biscay
  - Dolphins, whales (fin whale, pilot whale)
  - Basque coast: flysch geological formations
- Forests: beech, oak, chestnut
- Pyrenees (if route passes): brown bear, vultures, marmots, isard (Pyrenean chamois)

## SPAIN (Cantabrian coast, Basque Country)
- Cantabrian Mountains: last refuge of Cantabrian brown bear (~350 individuals)
  - Iberian wolf, Cantabrian chamois (rebeco), wild boar
  - Griffon vultures, Egyptian vultures, golden eagle
  - Somiedo Natural Park: bear observation
- Basque coast: flysch geological formations (60 million years)
  - Zumaia: spectacular layered rock exposing K-Pg boundary (dinosaur extinction)
- Picos de Europa: limestone massif, deep gorges
  - Chamois, golden eagle, Cantabrian capercaillie (critically endangered)
- Atlantic forest: oak, beech, chestnut (green Spain)
- Marine: Bay of Biscay whales, dolphins
- Asturias: "bear country", misty forested reserves
